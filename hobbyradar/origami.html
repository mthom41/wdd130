Origami

What is origami?

Origami is the ancient art of paper folding to create interesting new shapes and ideas. Thought to originate anciently from either China or Japan, Origami has both deep historical roots and impressive modern applications and advancements. Models range from pleasantly simple to mind-boggling and complex which provides an impressive range in which attracted hobbyists can begin learning. Simple models especially can be found as diagrams and instructions that can be followed at one’s leisure and own pace.

Accessibility

Origami has a very low barrier to entry. It’s very very cheap as well! The only thing you need to physically obtain is paper. Many books and videos are available online and at public libraries to help you move from beginner to advanced models. Origami can be done anywhere, anytime, and can be interrupted at pretty much any point in the folding process for you to go do important things or take whatever breaks you need.

Experience and Communities

I became fascinated by origami as a child around 8 or 9 years old and have phased in and out of passion for it ever since. It’s always a fun hobby to come back to, and if you fold some models enough you can pull them out as a cool party trick or a small gift for someone on short notice.

There are some awesome communities for origamists including local clubs, a couple of facebook pages and subreddits, tags on instagram and a plethora of websites with detailed instructions from beginner to very advanced models.
